The vast majority of students have never rented an apartment on their own before going to college. Students are unfamiliar with their rights as tenants, they don't know the reputations of the landlords in the area. Landy aims to increase transparency and help students make informed decisions when moving off-campus.

MY ROLE

Personal project


PROJECT DURATION

Ongoing

THE PROBLEM

When I was in school, most of the off-campus housing was owned by individuals rather than property management groups. This meant the service that students were receiving was very inconsistent, to the point where a subset of students refused to live on property that was not managed by our school.

The issue was three-part:

  • Students were repurposing social media platforms (Facebook, Reddit, and Twitter) to share their off-campus experiences
  • Students were inexperienced renters and did not know their rights as a tenant
  • There was no way to get additional information on landlords

OVERVIEW

Landy will be launched as a mobile application, on both iOS and Android. Many of the features will work best with mobile, so the mobile app will be prioritized over a website for desktop. Additionally smart phone usage is at an all-time high, with 94% of users age 18-29 owning a smart phone.

My vision is to empower college students to make informed decisions when moving off campus. The last thing students need to worry about is their living situation, when they should be focusing on their education.

The application will be monetized through university partnerships, and analytics provided to landlords . It is crucial that landlords and universities are not be able to pay to influence or change ratings, in order to the gain the trust of the students. Landlords will not be able to not be able to purchase advertisements, instead landlords are able to interact with the students to build credibility, while promoting themselves at the same time.

Universities have student living staff, and an off-campus dean that students can go to for assistance. Universities will be able to get a more accurate sense of the students' off-campus living experiences and intervene when necessary.

THE USERS

The users can be broken down into three segments: students, landlords, and universities. I will focus on the student user because this app will need an active community of students before landlords or universities will want to partner.

Students will be the primary users and will include both undergraduate and prospective students who have a strong preference for mobile-first. Prospective students will be researching universities, and will be interested in looking into off-campus housing. Graduate students will have more experience with renting apartments, but might be more cautious about who they rent from especially if they have a family.

Landlords will be an integral part of the community, and will include individual owners and property management groups. They want to get feedback from the students and grow their overall reputation.

Universities are the final type of user, and they want to ensure that their students are safe and comfortable during their studies.

NEXT STEPS


The next major steps would be development, monetization strategy, and launch plan. I would love to assemble a team of students or recent graduates who might be passionate about this issue. I am looking into developing it in react native, to get a MVP out to test with students. I want to launch in schools that are in rural college towns, where the majority of students are out-of-state residents. It is important that the school has mostly out-of-state students because it is a sure sign that they will have to find off-campus housing when attending university.

Rural areas have fewer properties owned by property management groups and more that are maintained by individuals. It is more difficult to get information on individual owners, and their service can be more inconsistent because they do not have company policies to abide by. To keep advertising costs down to a minimum, this application will be advertised on college specific Facebook groups and sub-Reddit pages, where students currently go to post their experiences. If a large student base uses this app, it would not be difficult to get landlords and universities using it as well. This is an ongoing personal project, and I hope to turn this into a reality someday very soon.
